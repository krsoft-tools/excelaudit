Excel does not provide a reliable audit trail for cell-level spreadsheet changes. Once data is overwritten, it can be difficult to prove what changed, when it changed, or whether the change history has been altered.

Why an Excel Audit Trail Matters

A proper audit trail helps you keep a trustworthy history of spreadsheet edits. This is especially useful when Excel files contain financial data, operational records, reconciliations, or other important business information.

Instead of relying on memory, manual checks, or weak version tracking, an audit trail gives you a clearer record of spreadsheet activity over time.

What ExcelAudit Records

ExcelAudit records cell-level modifications and stores change information in a structured audit log. This allows you to review what changed between spreadsheet versions and preserve a more reliable history of updates.

  • Changed cell values
  • Previous and new values
  • Changes across spreadsheet versions
  • Audit log continuity checks

How ExcelAudit Helps Protect the Audit Log

ExcelAudit uses a tamper-evident logging approach so that changes to the audit history itself can be detected. This helps reduce the risk of unnoticed manipulation and gives you stronger confidence in the stored record.
