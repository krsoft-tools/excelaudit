An Excel change log helps you keep a record of what was modified in a spreadsheet over time. This is useful when you need to review updates, verify changes, or understand how data evolved between versions.

Why a Change Log Matters

  • Spreadsheet edits are easy to miss
  • Old values are often overwritten permanently
  • Shared files may change without documentation
  • Manual review is slow and inconsistent

What an Excel Change Log Should Include

A useful change log should help you understand both the location and meaning of spreadsheet modifications.

  • Which cells changed
  • Old and new values
  • Sheet, row, and column references
  • A clear record of detected updates

How ExcelAudit Helps

ExcelAudit creates a structured Excel change log by recording detected modifications in a readable format. This makes it easier to review updates and keep a more reliable history of spreadsheet changes.

Instead of relying on memory or manual notes, you can work with a consistent record of what changed inside your files.
